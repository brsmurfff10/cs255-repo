# CS 255 Portfolio Reflection – DriverPass System Analysis and Design

## Brief summary of the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want me to design?

The DriverPass project focused on designing a web-based driver training system for the client, DriverPass. The goal was to create a platform where customers could purchase training packages, complete online practice tests, schedule driving lessons, and manage appointments. Administrators and instructors also needed tools to manage users, schedules, and lesson information. The project included creating a business requirements document and a complete system design using UML diagrams.

## What did I do particularly well?

I believe I did a good job translating the client's requirements into clear system models. Creating the UML diagrams helped me visualize how different users and system components interact. I also ensured that the business requirements and system design remained consistent throughout the project so that the proposed solution addressed the client's needs.

## If I could choose one part of my work on these documents to revise, what would I pick and how would I improve it?

If I were to revise one part of this project, I would improve the UML diagrams by adding additional detail and refining the layout. Although the diagrams accurately represented the system, better organization and spacing would make them easier to read. I would also include additional relationships and edge cases to provide a more complete representation of the system.

## How did I interpret the user's needs and implement them into my system design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?

I interpreted the user's needs by reviewing the client's interview and identifying the features that were most important to both customers and DriverPass staff. Those requirements guided the design of the classes, activities, and interactions shown throughout the UML diagrams. Considering user needs is essential because the system must solve real problems while remaining easy to use. Even a technically sound application will fail if it does not meet the expectations of the people using it.

## How do I approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would I use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I approach software design by first understanding the problem before thinking about implementation. Gathering requirements, identifying users, and defining the major system functions helps create a strong foundation. I then use UML diagrams to model the system from multiple perspectives before development begins. In future projects, I will continue using requirement analysis, object-oriented design principles, and iterative refinement to produce designs that are organized, maintainable, and aligned with user needs.
